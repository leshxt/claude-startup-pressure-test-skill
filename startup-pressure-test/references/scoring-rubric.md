# Startup Pressure Test — Scoring Rubric

## How to score each dimension (1–10)

### Problem Severity (Does this hurt enough?)
- 1-3: Nice-to-have. People complain but don't seek solutions.
- 4-6: Real pain, but manageable workarounds exist. Low urgency.
- 7-8: Recurring, costly pain with no great solution. Causes measurable loss (time/money/emotion).
- 9-10: Acute, mission-critical pain. People will pay anything to fix it.

### Market Size (How many people have this problem?)
- 1-3: Niche of a niche. < 10,000 potential customers globally.
- 4-6: Viable niche. 10k-500k customers. Profitable but not venture-scale.
- 7-8: Large addressable market. 500k-10M customers, or high-value B2B segment.
- 9-10: Mass market or dominant infrastructure play. >10M users or category-defining.

### Competitive Moat (What stops a well-funded competitor from copying this?)
- 1-3: No moat. Any dev can replicate in a weekend. No switching cost.
- 4-5: Mild friction (integrations, data accumulation, network effect seed).
- 6-7: Meaningful moat: proprietary data, regulatory, niche trust, or switching cost.
- 8-9: Strong moat: network effects, exclusive supply, deep switching cost.
- 10: Platform-level lock-in or regulatory monopoly.

### Founder Fit (Does this founder have an unfair advantage here?)
- 1-3: No relevant experience, no network, no domain knowledge.
- 4-6: General startup experience, tangential domain knowledge.
- 7-8: Direct domain expertise OR strong network in the target customer segment.
- 9-10: Deep unfair advantage: proprietary relationships, past operator in this exact space, technical moat.

Score N/A if no founder information is provided.

### Testability in 2 Weeks (Can the core assumption be falsified fast?)
- 1-3: Requires months of build, regulatory approval, or hardware.
- 4-6: Core UX can be tested but requires significant setup.
- 7-8: Landingpage + manual ops test validates core assumption quickly.
- 9-10: Core assumption testable with a spreadsheet, email, and a phone call.

---

## Verdict Thresholds

Average scorecard score:
- 7.0+: STRONG
- 5.0-6.9: WEAK (survivable, needs significant de-risking)
- <5.0: PIVOT

Override rules:
- If Problem Severity < 5: PIVOT regardless of other scores.
- If Testability < 4 and Competitive Moat < 5: WEAK minimum.
- If any Fatal Flaw is existential (regulatory, legal, physics): flag explicitly, may override to PIVOT.
